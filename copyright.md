## Prior Art Acknowledgment

This invention builds upon and is distinct from the following prior art:

1. **Conventional CSP tower technology** (single elevated receiver, wide heliostat field) — the VCST architecture eliminates the tower/receiver paradigm entirely.
2. **Parabolic trough CSP** (linear focus, evacuated tube receivers) — VCST uses point-focus heliostats with vertical rather than horizontal tube geometry.
3. **Published selective absorber coating research** (TiAlN, ZrC, HfMoN compounds) — VCST applies these coatings in a novel context (vertical tubes with concentrated solar flux) not described in the coating literature.
4. **sCO2 Brayton cycle research** (NREL Gen3 program) — VCST integrates sCO2 power conversion with a novel receiver geometry not present in the Gen3 roadmap.

The novel contribution is the specific combination of: (a) vertical tube cluster geometry, (b) close-range compact heliostat field, (c) spectrally selective coatings optimized for the resulting flux/temperature regime, and (d) integrated counter-flow fluid delivery through hollow tube cores, together achieving land-use energy density exceeding conventional PV and CSP.
